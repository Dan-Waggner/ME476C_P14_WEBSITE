Aside from the awesome people listed under the "About Us" section, this project also featured the NAU Rocket Club and NAU's Dr. Carson Pete as the team's primary clients and stakeholders. More information about both Dr. Carson Pete and the NAU Rocket Club can be found below.

The team was tasked to create a rocket payload capable of performing various of actions based on received RF inputs sent by NASA for the 2023 NASA Student Launch competition. This payload needed to be able to apply various filters to a camera system which was normal to the direction of gravity and was capable of rotating 180 degrees.

On an academic basis, this project was conducted in order to further prepare the team members for industry standards regarding engineering and design. On a scientific basis, this project was done to replicate the necessary processes NASA interplanetary probes are tasked with completing upon landing on an interstellar body.

Carson Pete

NAU Professor Carson Pete was one of the team's clients. He provided us with important information, pointers, and recommendations for the project as a whole.

NAU Rocket Club

Acting as both a client and a stakeholder, the Capstone team worked directly with the NAU Rocket Club throughout the entirety of this project.
