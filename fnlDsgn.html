Final Design Prototype

The prototype created by the team served to demonstrate the feasibility of a mechanical gimbal and the proposed method of deployment. The assembly process was also subsequently reevaluated in order to completely remove the usage of adhesives, as adhesive materials caused the ball bearings to be misaligned, resulting in the gimbal having issues rotating with the dummy weight used.

Future iterations are likely to see the outer gimbal platform being composed of metal (likely aluminum), with the ball bearings being press-fitted into the platform and into the gimbal base caps. This will remove any possibility of misalignment caused by the use of adhesives. Additionally, the various wooden dowels shown will be replaced with 0.25 in metal rods (also aluminum), which can be purchased from various online vendors for very little. Preliminary research suggests that 2x 1 foot long 0.25 inch Aluminum Rods can be purchased for as little as $3 USD before shipping. These rods will also likely be press-fitted into their respective slots.
